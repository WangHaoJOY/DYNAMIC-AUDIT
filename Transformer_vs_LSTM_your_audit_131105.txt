### Detailed Explanation of the Transformer Architecture and Comparison with LSTM

#### 1. Self-Attention Mechanism

**Explanation:**
Self-attention is a mechanism where each token in the input sequence pays attention to all other tokens, including itself, to generate its contextual embedding [1][2]. This allows the model to consider the entire sequence simultaneously rather than sequentially processing it.

- **Source:** 
  - [1] Transformer Architecture Explained With Self-Attention Mechanism
  - [2] The Transformer Architecture and the Power of Self-Attention - Medium

**Detailed Explanation:**
In self-attention, each token in a sequence calculates a weighted sum of all tokens' representations based on their relevance. This is done using three key components:
1. **Query (Q):** A representation that captures what information from other tokens is needed.
2. **Key (K):** A representation that captures the presence or absence of specific information within each token.
3. **Value (V):** The actual value that will be used to generate context.

The attention score for a token `i` regarding token `j` is computed as:
\[ \text{score}(i, j) = \frac{\exp(Q_i^T K_j)}{\sum_k \exp(Q_i^T K_k)} \]

The final output is the weighted sum of values:
\[ \text{output}_i = \sum_j \text{score}(i, j) V_j \]

- [4] 11. Attention Mechanisms and Transformers - Dive into Deep Learning

#### 2. Role of Positional Encoding

**Explanation:**
Positional encoding adds important positional information to each token, enabling the model to understand the relationships and order of tokens despite their sequential nature [1][2]. This is crucial for capturing sequential dependencies in a non-sequential manner.

- **Source:** 
  - [1] Positional Encoding Explained: A Deep Dive into Transformer PE
  - [2] Positional Encoding in Transformers - GeeksforGeeks

**Detailed Explanation:**
Positional encoding can be added as sinusoidal functions of the token's position:
\[ \text{PE}_{(pos,2i)} = \sin\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{model}}}\right) \]
\[ \text{PE}_{(pos,2i+1)} = \cos\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{model}}}\right) \]

This ensures that each token has a unique encoding based on its position in the sequence. The model can then combine this positional information with the learned representations.
