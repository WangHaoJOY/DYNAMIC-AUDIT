### 3-Day Free Trip to Tokyo Itinerary

#### Day 1: Exploring Shinjuku and Nearby Areas

**Morning:**
- **Start at Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden:** A peaceful oasis with three distinct gardens (English-speaking guides available). [1]
- **Transportation:** Walk from your accommodation or take the JR Line to Shinjuku Station, then walk 5 minutes to the garden.
- **Lunch Suggestion:** Try a "Yakitori" place in Shinjuku, like Kameya Yakitori. This is a local favorite for grilled chicken skewers.

**Afternoon:**
- **Visit Marunouchi and Kabukicho (Red Light District):** Explore the neon-lit streets with shops, bars, and restaurants.
- **Transportation:** Walk from Shinjuku to Marunouchi via the Yamanote Line or walk 10 minutes. Kabukicho is about a 20-minute walk from Shinjuku Station.

**Evening:**
- **Dinner at Sushi Dai in Tsukishima:** A popular spot for sushi, though it can get very crowded.
- **Transportation:** Take the Yamanote Line to Tsukishima Station and walk 15 minutes. Alternatively, take a taxi if you prefer.

#### Day 2: Shibuya and Harajuku

**Morning:**
- **Start at Shibuya Crossing (Shibuya Hachiko Statue):** Famous for being one of the busiest pedestrian crossings in the world.
- **Transportation:** Take the JR Line or Tokyo Metro to Shibuya Station, which is a 10-minute walk from Shinjuku.

**Afternoon:**
- **Visit Harajuku and Takeshita Street:** Known for its colorful fashion, cafes, and street art.
- **Transportation:** Take the JR Line to Harajuku Station (5 minutes).

**Evening:**
- **Dinner at Ichiran Ramen in Shibuya:** A popular ramen chain with a unique single-pot serving method. [2]
- **Transportation:** Walk 10 minutes from Harajuku Station.

#### Day 3: Tokyo Tower and Ueno

**Morning:**
- **Visit Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building (Tokyo Tower):** Enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- **Transportation:** Take the JR Line to Shibuya, then transfer to the Chiyoda Line to Shinjuku. Walk from Shinjuku Station to Tokyo Tower.

**Afternoon:**
- **Explore Ueno Park and Ueno Zoo:** A great place for relaxing in a park setting.
- **Transportation:** Take the Yamanote Line to Ueno Station (30 minutes).

**Evening:**
- **Dinner at Torikizoku in Asakusa:** Known for its traditional Japanese cuisine. [1]
- **Transportation:** Take the Tokyo Metro to Asakusa Station, then walk 20 minutes.

### Transportation Suggestions
- **Subway/Walking:** Prioritize using the subway system (Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway) as they are efficient and cover most areas of interest.
- **Taxis/Buses:** Useful for last-minute travel or if you find yourself in a more remote area. Taxis can be pre-booked online for convenience.

### Accommodation Recommendations
- **Near Shinjuku or Shibuya:**
  - **Hotel Shirokanedai Roppongi Hills**: A modern, centrally located hotel with reasonable prices.
  - **Shibuya Granbell Hotel Tokyo**: Offers good value and proximity to major attractions in Shibuya.

### Food Recommendations
- **Breakfast:** Try "Ramen" at local izakayas like Mihara in Shinjuku or Yakiniku Rokuyon in Shibuya.
- **Lunch/Dinner:** Sushi Dai (Shinjuku), Ichiran Ramen (Shibuya), Torikizoku (Asakusa).
