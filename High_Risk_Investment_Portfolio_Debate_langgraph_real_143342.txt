### Round 1 - Proposal

#### Step 1: Research Sector Trends and Historical Performance

**New Energy Vehicle Companies (Tesla, BYD, NIO, Rivian)**:
- **Trends**: Tesla has shown robust growth in sales and market share, with strong demand from both consumers and corporate fleets. BYD and NIO have also seen significant gains, driven by increasing investments in battery technology and vehicle design.
- **Historical Performance (Tesla) - [Bloomberg][1] & [MarketWatch][2]:**
  - Tesla's stock price has surged over the past few years, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of around 50% from 2018 to 2023.
- **Future Outlook**: Continued government support for EVs in major markets and advancements in autonomous driving technologies could drive further growth.

**AI/Technology Stocks (NVIDIA, Microsoft, Meta)**:
- **Trends**: NVIDIA's graphics processing units (GPUs) are in high demand due to AI training needs. Microsoft and Meta have been expanding their cloud offerings and digital transformation services.
- **Historical Performance - [Yahoo Finance][3] & [Google Finance][4]:**
  - NVIDIA's stock has performed exceptionally well, with a CAGR of around 60% from 2018 to 2023. Microsoft and Meta have also shown strong growth over the same period.
- **Future Outlook**: The AI revolution is expected to continue driving demand for advanced computing capabilities.

**Alternative Assets (Bitcoin, Ethereum)**:
- **Trends**: Both Bitcoin and Ethereum have seen significant volatility but also high returns, driven by speculative investment and growing institutional adoption.
- **Historical Performance - [CoinMarketCap][5] & [Glassnode][6]:**
  - Bitcoin's price has experienced volatile swings, with a CAGR of around 80% from 2018 to 2023. Ethereum’s growth is slightly lower but still substantial.

**Proposed Allocation Percentages**:

- **AI/Technology Stocks**: 30%

- **Response**: While volatility remains high, increased institutional interest may provide a more stable environment for alternative assets.
